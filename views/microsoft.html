Design Internship at Microsoft

During the summer of 2016, I was a UI/UX Design Intern in the Microsoft Garage internship program based in Cambridge, Massachusetts. In accordance with the entrepreneurial spirit of the internship, we were tasked with shipping a project in just 12 weeks. I worked on two teams, each consisting of 6 developers and myself. Throughout the internship, we practiced an agile scrum development process, which included weekly sprint planning, careful feature prioritization, and constant iteration.

Project Santorini


PROJECT OVERVIEW

Project Santorini is a unique photo experience that evokes nostalgia and the thrill of re-discovery. An established group at Microsoft was interested in creating a new and innovative photo experience, and they asked us to build it. Under the guidance of that group and the directors of the Garage internship, we brought Project Santorini from a concept to a shipped Windows app in 3 months. The name "Project Santorini" is a temporary codename while the app is still in beta.


MY ROLE

Concept ideation, creating user workflows, prototyping interaction, designing wireframes and high fidelity mockups, user research and testing.


USER RESEARCH

Since we were given such a broad prompt, we started by conducting user research in order to better understand the current photos space. Through our user research interviews, we learned that though there are many useful photo organizing and editing apps available, unmet user needs still remain. Here's what people told us:


WHAT WE LEARNED

  • People struggle to feel emotionally connected to their digital photos
  • Because it's so easy to snap photos with digital devices, people often have large photo libraries that they rarely ever revisit
  • Many people feel overwhelmed by the sheer size of their photo library
  • Seeing old photos evokes positive moments of nostalgia and reflection


IDEATION

"What exists in the photo space already? What can we add to the space?"

We considered:

  • How can we help people connect with their past memories in a delightful and compelling way?
  • How can we push boundaries in the existing photos space?
  • How will can we clearly communicate a new photo concept to our stakeholders and garner their support?
